Reflective Experience & Integration

Spending quiet, focused time contemplating my temple covenants was a deeply comforting experience that brought me immense clarity. In the peaceful atmosphere of the temple, away from the busyness of school assignments and technical labs, I took the time to slow down, pray, and write down my thoughts on where the Lord is leading me. This quiet reflection helped me realize that our spiritual lives are not separate from our professional work[cite: 78, 105]. They are tightly connected[cite: 81]. In cybersecurity, we talk about building strong perimeters, checking credentials, and keeping out unauthorized access to protect sensitive systems. This is very similar to how the temple acts as a sacred, secure space on Earth, where we must prepare and align our lives with the Lord’s standards before we enter.

This reflection reminded me of Elder David A. Bednar's analogy of the intertwined strands of a rope. My technical skills, career aspirations, faith in Jesus Christ, and ethical integrity should not exist as separate, isolated parts of my life[cite: 78]. Instead, they must be woven together to make me a stronger person. When I approach my cybersecurity studies as a way to serve others and keep them safe, my schoolwork becomes a form of worship[cite: 52, 81]. The peace I felt in the temple has renewed my commitment to make my future career a reflection of my covenants—acting with complete honesty, serving my coworkers and clients, and relying on Heavenly Father to guide my path[cite: 48, 56, 129].
